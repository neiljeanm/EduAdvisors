The Problem

AI is already in the classroom. Nationwide, 84% of U.S. high school students* and 60% of K–12 teachers** use AI tools in some capacity.

Yet nearly half of U.S. school districts either:

  • Have no formal AI policy, or
  • Leave decisions up to individual schools and teachers

Most administrators and educators don’t have the time or technical background to create district-wide AI policies — let alone ensure alignment with state or county guidance.

Schools that don't have AI policies and guidelines in place face a number of potential risks including:

  • Data privacy violations
  • Student exposure to inaccurate or harmful content
  • Plagiarism and academic integrity issues
  • Legal liability
  • Staff and administrator confusion about what is or isn’t permitted
  • Loss of community trust
